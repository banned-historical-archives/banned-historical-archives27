Panama's 505-member legislative assembly today elects former Minister of Education Aristides Royo--General Torrijos' personal choice--as president for a six-year term. Torrijos will remain commandant of the National Guard but will relinquish his post as Chief of Government, a position that will be abolished. A still unanswered question is to what extent Torrijos will relax his grip on the levers of power and allow Royo--ambitious but to date of unquestioned loyalty to Torrijos-- to be his own man. Royo, although a leftist nationalist, is primarily a pragmatist. He faces an economy just beginning to emerge from extended stagnation; in order to encourage recovery he will have to convince businessmen that he is a moderate. He will also pre- side over political reforms--the full extent of which are still not clear--that Torrijos has initiated to allow greater partici- pation by the opposition.
