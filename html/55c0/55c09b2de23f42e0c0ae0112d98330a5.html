TUTTLE COMPANY Publishers and Booksellers RUTLAND, VERMONT You'd be surprised at the success of such off-beat titles as THE JAPANESE ABACUS, the poor man's IBM; ZEN FLESH, ZEN BONES, a collection of actual Zen writings; and THE GAME OF GO, the Orient's challenge to checkers. Public response to MODERN JAPANESE PRINTS proved that the American art-lover can appreciate this avant-garde art form, even though it lacks the expected in "Oriental" art. The inexpensive, lavishly illustrated LIBRARY OF JAPANESE ART has more than fulfilled expectations on the American book market, and we were delighted at the enthusiastic response to the volume on Sesshu, whose main works were in the black and white ink-painting tradition, rather than the more colorful genre school. An interesting incident concerning one of our forthcoming publi- cations, THE HOKUSAI SKETCHBOOKS, took place when the plane in which the author, James Michener, carrying the manuscript with him, was forced to ditch in the middle of the Pacific Ocean. Fortunately, Michener succeeded in reconstructing his text from notes and from his prodigious memory. We are pleased to report that reprints of several Oriental classics, including THE BOOK OF TEA and Lafcadio Hearn's JAPAN: AN INTERPRETATION have done well.
