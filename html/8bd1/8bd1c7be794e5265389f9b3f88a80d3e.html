The USSR, how- ever, would approve only temporary, limited East German measures designed to underscore East German and Soviet disapproval because it has more important interests, in- cluding the forthcoming summit. East Germany has long taken the position that West Berlin is a separate political entity from West Germany and has bitterly opposed any actions that imply otherwise. 25X1 Aside from generally impeding access to West Berlin, East Germany could ban travel by West Berliners elected to the European Parliament on the surface access routes. The East Germans in the past have stopped West German of- ficials from using the surface access routes to under- score their displeasure about West Germany increasing its political ties with West Berlin. The East Germans could also further limit one-day visits that West Berliners cur- rently are allowed to East Berlin and increase currency exchange requirements for West Germans traveling in East East Germany probably would prefer severe measures to dramatize its position, but it is doubtful that the USSR will let the East Germans upset East-West relations at a time when the SALT II treaty is ready for signature and faces an uncertain fate in the US Senate. Even small infringements on travel to and from West Berlin, however, would violate the Four-Power Agreement on Berlin and prob- ably lead the West Germans to request an Allied protest. She was elected to a seat in the lower house of the Indian parliament last November but was expelled a month later for alleged corrupt practices. Her supporters are planning to hold a large public rally in New Delhi tomorrow on her behalf. COMMENT: Gandhi probably would run only if she felt as- sured of an easy victory. She reportedly had been in- clined to run next fall for her former seat from the state of Karnataka adjacent to Tamil Nadu. The Egyptian masses are still overwhelm- ingly behind President Sadat and the treaty, but support from the elite is beginning to erode. Those Egyptians think in terms of Cairo having lost a leadership role in the Arab world and are "deeply disturbed" about what is happening around them. Growing Egyptian misgivings have not produced any significant attacks on US policy so far; most interlocutors of the Embassy, Eilts noted, still ac- cept that the US, especially President Carter, genuinely is seeking a just, durable peace. China 25X1 Analyzing China's leadership meetings of the past two months, the US Embassy in Beijing reports that the maneuvering seems to have preserved rivalries intact and only papered over many of the controversies within the leadership. Diplomats in Beijing generally believe that Vice Premier Deng Xiaoping was taken down a few notches at the meetings--a view which seems to be supported by most outward signs--and that important voices other than those of Deng and Chairman Hua Guofeng are making them- selves heard. No new issues were introduced, and Trudeau, an acknowledged master of such face-to-face sparring, failed to land any "mortal wounds" on Clark in the opinion of the Embassy. Even Clark's campaign workers, the Embassy noted, had feared he would disgrace the party in some irredeemable fashion during the debate and thereby throw away a possible election victory. The third debater, New Democratic Party leader Ed Broadbent, declined to say what his party would do should neither of the leading parties gain a majority of the seats, a distinct possibility in what appears to be a close con- test.
