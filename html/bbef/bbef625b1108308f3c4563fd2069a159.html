One of the traditional key status symbols is the number of honorary nominations for election to the Supreme Soviet reported in Pravda on the first day of nominations for each Politburo member. Brezhnev's lead, while substantial, has not widened since 1974. Premier Kosygin ranks second with the same number of nominations as be- fore. The choice of honorific titles might be less tightly controlled than the number of honorary nominations accorded each "candidate" and thus open to political manipulation.
