On paper, the election made little difference to the balance of power. Prime Minister Suarez again fell short of a majority and, though he could govern with floating majorities, he will probably continue to seek consensus on most important issues. Nevertheless, the relatively small shifts that did occur are significant and Suarez is clearly in a stronger position for dealing with his chief rivals, the Socialists. Suarez won this advantage-- and a four-year extension of his mandate--at the cost of lengthy delays in implementing vital legislation--most notably on the economy and on rules governing regional autonomy.
