The Publications Distribution Agency, controlled. by the North Korean postal 25X1 administration, functions as the central distributor of government-approved newspapers and. magazines throughout North Korea. This agency accepts sub- scriptions and is responsible for the delivery of the publications to the subscribers.1 The political security unit of government agencies is also authorized to accept subscriptions which are then turned. in to the Publication Distribution Agency for processing. 2. The major North Korean newspapers are published in limited quantities. Each bureau of the Ministry of Internal Affairs receives only four copies of the officially sanctioned newspapers. Each of the principal railroad stations receives a few copies which are sold chiefly to traveling govern- ment officials. The majority of the bookstores in North Korea are operated by the government and are stocked with books of Soviet or North Korean origin. Some book- stores are privately owned and operated and deal in second-hand books, most of which are medical or engineering reference books printed in Japanese. The following books are required reading for all government officials and are currently being used as manuals for the indoctrination of government employees and KLP members in Party theory and dogma. Persons receiving government support are also required to read these books. The general public shows little interest in them, partly because of the dull subject matter and partly because of the tedious and ponderous style in which they are written.
