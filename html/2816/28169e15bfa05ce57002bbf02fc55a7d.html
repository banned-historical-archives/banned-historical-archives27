Thich Ho Giac apparently addressed some 2,000 persons at the Buddhist Institute today urging them to give the Directorate until Monday to add ten civilian members to it, according to press reports. Signs of opposition to Ho Giac's statement of acceptance were noted, however, Some members of the Buddhist Monks and Nuns Association reportedly condemned the move and indicated more suicides as a protest. During a funeral procession for two im- molated Buddhists led by Tam Chau today, marchers re- portedly beat a plainclothes security agent but did not demonstrate. In the opinion of General Khang, one of the more mil- itant Directorate members, the Buddhists are suffering from a "severe" power struggle and are losing popular support.
