The record harvest in 1970 represents a significant payoff from the regime's "agriculture firs~;~' policies* adopted beginni:lg in 1962. Such measures as (a) increased investment in industries producing inputs for agriculture, (b) regular im- ports of chemical fertilizers, and (c) the con- centration of these inputs on the mast productive farmland have enabled the Chinese to increase grain production at a rate commensurate with the growth in population. Agricultural productivity has also been enhanced by the avoidance of policies further restricting peasant incentives. In particular the regime abandoned measures adopted during the Leap Forward restricting private enterprise and now permits individual initiative in the cultivation of private plots and actually encourages the private raising of hogs and poultry.
