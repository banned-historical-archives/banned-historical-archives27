"A" IRAN. Iranians Propose Nationalization of the Iranian-Soviet Fisheries. Prime Minister Ala has informed US Ambassador Grady that Parliament plans to nationalize the Caspian fisheries on expiration of the Soviet con- cession in September 1952. Several Parliamentary deputies are now drawing up a bill on this subject and will introduce it shortly in Parlia- ment. (S Tehran 2474, 19 Apr 51). COMMENT: The Caspian fisheries are owned jointly by the Iranian and Soviet Governments with each government having 50% of the shares. The terms of the contract permit the Iranian government, if it does not wish to renew the contract at its expiration date, to dissolve the company and to divide the assets between the two share-holders. Under such circumstances the Iranians must exploit the fisheries themselves for a period of 25 years without the assistance of non-Soviet foreign nationals. The Iranian Government has long planned to do this. While the move might be interpreted as an extension of the current fervor for nationalization of foreign concessions in Iran, it is not comparable to the move.to nationalize the Anglo-Iranian Oil Company (AIOC), which is envisaged as taking place immediately rather than when the AIOC contract expires in 1993.
