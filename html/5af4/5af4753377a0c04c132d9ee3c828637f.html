Elections The general elections and the beginning of the Five-Year Plan took place in 1953 as a result of the unprecedented reconstruction, production, and consoli- dation of power in the hand] of the working classes. The possibility of politically mobilizing almost 350 million electors and of technically organizing their vote, even in the most remot- villages of the hinterland and the mountains, is the result not only of reorganizing the nation's civic life but also of the intense political activity which has taken place lately for the first time among the popular masses in their political and mass organizations.
