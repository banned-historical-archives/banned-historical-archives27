On the following day, the governor accepted two additional demands put forward by Chinese elements in Macao. Peking has not yet publicly commented on the Portuguese capit- ulation. However, the unofficial Chinese Communist representative in Macao has indicated his satis- faction with the governor's statement, and four Chinese Communist gunboats reportedly withdrew, suggesting that Peking intends to relax its pressure for the present. The display of Portuguese inability and unwillingness to resist Chinese Communist pressure makes it clear that Peking can at any time score further cheap and easy political victories at Portugal's expense. For the mo- ment, however, Communist China will probably be satisfied with the acknowledgment by the Macao authorities that Peking has the predominant role in respect to Chinese citizens of Macao,. None- theless, unplanned incidents which might provoke riots and renewed Chinese intervention cannot be ruled out.
