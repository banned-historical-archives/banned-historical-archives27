The central reference facilities of the Office of Central Reference (the CIA Library, Biographic, Graphics and Industrial Registers in particular) were developed and are being constantly improved to service this need. Research work generally begins with a survey of available books, periodicals, reports, docu- ments and other analytical material. One of the time-proven tools for surveying literature on a given subject or of a given period is the bibliography. Numerous compendia have been prepared specifically for intelligence needs, and many others have been done by U. S. Govern- ment agencies of related value to intelligence. To date there has been no comprehensive guide to these resources, and no basic reference tool to which an intelligence analyst may turn with assurance of assistance. This GUIDE represents a major effort on the part of the CIA Library to meet this need and to serve as a supplement to the facilities mentioned above. A draft of the GUIDE was prepared and submitted to appropriate individuals in the intelligence community for comment and suggestions; this final form reflects their contributions. Bibliographies and indexes published since World War II have been emphasized; items published before 1945 have been included if their coverage of a particular subject was extensive or if no later compilation was made. Items classified through SECRET are listed. Each section includes basic research aids selected by the CIA Library staff as the best bibliographic tool available in the field. The entries are arranged by subject, and then by area. Within the subject classes, a general category is listed first, with area categories following in alphabetical order. Annual and other serial publications are listed. Approximate dates have been supplied for items for which the initial date of publication could not be determined. Each entry has been assigned an entry number, formed by assigning a letter to each section and numbering the entries consecutively within the section. There are accompanying indexes by area, by subject, by source, and by title and series to facilitate the location of a specific item dealing with a particular subject or area. Questions arising from the use of this GUIDE should be directed to the CIA Librarian.
