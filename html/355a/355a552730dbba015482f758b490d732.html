COMPUTER DEVELOPMENT IN POLAND -- Warsaw, Przeglad Techniczny, No 35, 31 Aug 60, P 7 CPYRGHT Polish specialists from the Mathematical Apparatus Plant of the Polish Academy of Sciences are working on "Skrzat," a mathematical com- puter system which will control certain processes at the "Jedlicze" refinery. The machine will control such conditions as temperature, pressure, and rate of flow automatically. At the same time, the Mathematical Apparatus Plant is working on "ZAM - 3," a mathematical calculating machine based on a ferrite- semiconductor system. The machine now under construction, which will approach the world's leading machines in operating speed, will be used by the Polish National Bank. The plant is also working on several types of analog computers, such as "EMIRR" and "ELI," which will solve ordinary and fractional differential equations. Work is also continuing on the "SAKO" system, which is based on automatic preparation or programming of calculating machines for solving specific problems. Members of the force receive specialized training enabling them to detect border violators and smugglers of goods.
