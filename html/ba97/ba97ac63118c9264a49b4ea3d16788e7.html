YU ASSERTED THAT WHAT THE CHINESE NEED TODAY IS THE LEGITIMACY OF THE THREE PRINCIPLES OF THE PEOPLE, NOT COMMUNISM. U.S. WILL NOT PRESSURE TAIWAN TO HOLD PEACE TALKS WITH PRC: - SEVERAL PAPERS, CNA WASHINGTON: U.S. ADMINISTRATION OFFICIALS SAID THAT THE U.S. GOVERNMENT WILL NOT PUT PRESSURE ON TAIWAN TO CONDUCT PEACE TALKS WITH THE PRC. THE REMARKS WERE IN RESPONSE TO A STATEMENT MADE BY PREMIER YU AT THE LY SESSION ON MARCH 11, IN WHICH HE SAID THAT "EVEN IF THERE IS PRESSURE FROM THE U.S., THE ROC WILL NOT NEGOTIATE WITH THE CHINESE COMMUNISTS." THE AMERICAN OFFICIALS NOTED THAT ACCORDING TO THE WASHINGTON-BEIJING COMMUNIQUE SIGNED IN JULY 1982, THE U.S. HAS PROMISED NOT TO ACT AS AN INTERMEDIARY BETWEEN TAIPEI AND BEIJING AND WILL NOT BRING ANY PRESSURE ON TAIWAN TO NEGOTIATE WITH THE PRC. ON MARCH 13 THE DEPUTY WHITE HOUSE SPOKESMAN REFUSED TO COMMENT ON THE PREMIER'S REMARKS EXCEPT TO NOTE THAT "NO COMMENT" SHOULD NOT BE INTERPRETED AS A CHANGE IN THE U.S. ASSURANCE TO TAIWAN. - INDEPENDENCE EVENING POST, FRONT PAGE HEADLINES, MARCH 14; ALL PAPERS, FRONT PAGE REPORTS: OPPOSITION LEGISLATOR HSU JUNG-SHU SUBMITTED AN INTERPELLATION IN WHICH SHE SAID THAT NEARLY 20,000 COPIES OF HER MAGAZINE, "SENH-KIN," WERE SUDDENLY CONFISCATED ON THE NIGHT OF MARCH 12 WITHOUT A WARRANT FROM THE COURT. SOONG ARGUED THAT THE POLICE DID OBTAIN A WARRANT TO SEARCH THE PREMISES TOGETHER WITH THE CULTURAL SCREENING TASK FORCE. PREMIER YU SAID LATER THAT THE DEFENSE MINISTER HAD MADE A SLIP OF THE TONGUE; HE WAS REALLY REFERRING TO THE EXTREMELY SMALL NUMBER OF PEOPLE WHO USE THE SLOGAN OF "DEMOCRACY" WHEN WHAT THEY WANT IS HARMONY WITH THE PRC, PEACE TALKS, AND DISCORD AMONG THE PEOPLE HERE. YU STRESSED THAT DEMOCRACY IS THE COMMON ASPIRATION OF THE KMT AND THE OPPOSITION, AND THAT RESPECT FOR DEMOCRATIC FREEDOM, THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THE CONSTITUTION AND THE RULE OF LAW ARE PART OF THE FOUNDING SPIRIT OF THE NATION. - SEVERAL PAPERS: GIO DIRECTOR GENERAL CHANG KING-YUH, ECHOING THE DEFENSE MINISTER'S RESPONSE, TOLD THE LY THAT THE ROC IS A NATION WITH AMPLE FREEDOM OF SPEECH AND THAT THERE WILL BE NO "CONTAINMENT" OF SPEECH HERE. AS AN EXAMPLE, CHANG CITED THE MASS MEDIA'S EXTENSIVE COVERAGE AND ANALYSIS OF INTERPELLATIONS RAISED BY OPPOSITION LAWMAKERS AND THE CABINET'S RESPONSES. HE ADDED THAT DIFFERENCES BETWEEN THE REPORTS AND THE LEGISLATIVE INTERPELLATION ARE A REFLECTION OF FREEDOM OF SPEECH IN TAIWAN; IT SHOULD NOT BE INTERPRETED AS "CONTAINMENT." - SOME PAPERS: ON THE LAST DAY OF THE JOINT LY INTERPELLATION SESSION YESTERDAY, THREE PRO-KMT LEGISLATORS, YANG PAO-LIN, CHOU SHU-FU AND CHANG HUNG- SHUEH, OBJECTED TO THE OPPOSITION'S COMMENTS ON THE POLITICAL CRISES, INCLUDING STRONG MAN RULE, THE LIFTING OF MARTIAL LAW, AND THE RUMOR THAT "CHIANG HSIAO-WU'S APPOINTMENT TO SINGAPORE AS DEPUTY TRADE REPRESENTATIVE IS A PEACE OVERTURE TO THE PRC". THEY TERMED THE COMMENTS FAR-FETCHED AND INAPPROPRIATE.
