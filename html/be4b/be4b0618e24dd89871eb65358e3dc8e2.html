Declassified in Part - Sanitized Copy Approved for Release 2012/05/22 : CIA-RDP87-01104R000100110004-6 UNITED STATES FORCES, JAPAN FOREIGN BROADCAST INFORMATION SERVICE OKINAWA BUREAU APO SAN FRANCISCO 98239 MOK-1987 6 October 1986 MEMORANDUM FOR: Director, Foreign Broadcast Information Service Chief, Operations Group, FBIS Chief, Okinawa Bureau, FBIS STAT SUBJECT: Monthly Report - Okinawa Bureau - September 1986 Maj. Gen. Edwin J. Godfrey has been named to replace Maj. Gen. James L. Day as Okinawa Area Coordinator for U.S. Forces Japan, the senior military post on the island. General Day will retire in early November. Godfrey is also commanding general of III Marine Amphibious Forces/3rd Marine Division. Brig. Gen. Henry Stackpole, who is Godfrey's deputy, will assume Day's duties as commander of Marine Corps Base Camp Butler. A. Monitorial/Editorial 1. On 18 September the bureau assumed live coverage of Beijing television from Hong Kong bureau where it had been done on a delayed basis from recordings. Hong Kong monitor visited Okinawa from 17 September through 2 October to assist in the coverage transition. 2. Observation of the Soviet 140 East satellite normally used to relay Moscow TV to the Soviet Far East has confirmed a pattern of frequent feeds of international television from North Korea, the PRC and use in "telebridges" involving the United States, after the closedown of normal Moscow TV transmissions. Several videotapes were forwarded to MOD from these feeds. Feeds on six separate occasions included a discussion by satellite between Soviet and U.S. scientists on nuclear power stations and the nuclear winter threat and reportage on visiting Polish, Czechoslovak and Nicaraguan government delegations to North Korea and the PRC. These transmissions are clearly part of the communist Intervision network. 3. The third Chinese monitor recruited this year, arrived on 11 September to begin training, while the secon , who joined FBIS in August, r ned after one month and returned to Taiwan. Senior monitor resigned to retire in mid-November. While one more monitor is slated to EOD in December, we have already scheduled another recruiting trip for late this year. Hanoi domestic voice coverage was transferred on 4 September to Bangkok. Okinawa retains coverage of only the VNA English press agency and Moscow and Beijing Vietnamese commentary programs. 5. Major Chinese coverage included Beijing's handling of a CBS "60 Minutes" Mike Wallace interview with Deng Xiaoping and the sixth plenary session of the CPC Central Committee. The latter was marred by the loss of Xinhua English resulting from the first major propagation dropout this fall right in the middle of the lengthy guidelines adopted at the session. London Bureau quickly filed the middle two-thirds of the item. The bureau also provided extensive VSL reportage on TV handling of the plenum. B. Cruising In addition to observing the Soviet 140 East satellite for unscheduled TV feeds, cruisers caught the first intercept from Urumchi's Xinjiang domestic television in Uighur and Mandarin on the 66 East Intelsat. Tapes were forwarded to Headquarters. Two Indian television services were observed with very poor reception on the 74 East Intelsat. Checks on all satellite transponders within Okinawa's look angle were completed prior to starting Beijing satellite TV coverage, which requires at least 4 hours of 10-meter dish time every evening. Seoul Bureau Korean monitor visited at Okinawa 7-13 September for cruising training. At Seoul Bureau's request, Okinawa dubbed an extra copy of a Moscow TV press conference featuring a North Korean military attache describing transfer of 150,000 KPA troops to civil construction duties. In response to a consumer request, the bureau obtained and sent to Headquarters by DHL courier an article in a Tokyo weekly, Shukan Posuto, not on bureau coverage. 1. Most of the damage to bureau antennas by Typhoon Vera has been repaired. Rotten poles--due for replacement next fiscal year--prevented climbing by technicians. The Pesos satellite dish was secured with two additional guywires against further typhoon damage. 2. Marked improvement in the quality of Beijing TV was evident after new ROSET mainframe down converter equipment was put into operation. 3. Equipment for the new bureau emergency radio network was ordered with FY-86 funds. The network will have base stations at Kadena and Bolo Point, 6 hand-held and 4 car transceivers, each with two usable frequencies, one for use only by FBIS and one shared by military units, especially during typhoons. He received flowers from the communications section, a framed letter from the Director FBIS, the FBIS Career Medal, and a framed certificate of appreciation from the Kadena Base Commander. The new communications supervisor is a teletypist since 1966. B. The exchange rate remained just above 150 yen to the dollar throughout the month. On 1 October the base exchange raised gasoline prices to $1.069 per self-serve gallon, citing higher local labor costs. Japanese media continue to report a possible further decline of the dollar, and commentators were apprehensive about the impact on small Japanese companies dependent on export trade. C.
