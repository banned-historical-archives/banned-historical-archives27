Approved For Release 2008/04/01 :CIA-RDP86-000408000100050009-1 FOREIGN BROADCAST INFORMATION SERVICE HONG KONG BUREAU MHK--0048 3 March 1982 MEMORANDUM FOR: Director, Foreign Broadcast Information Service THROUGH: Chief, Operations Group SUBJECT: Monthly Report--Hong Kong Bureau--February 1982 The bureau chief met on 12 February with two visiting IC staff members fora briefing on bureau operations. They were particularly interested in the effects of the FSN classification system on the bureau (having obviously gotten an earful before hitting Hong Kong). The problems of finding and retaining qualified Mandarin speakers in Hong Kong and the difficulties of competing with private industry were discussed. They expressed the opinion that FBIS performed a very valuable service on a small budget and wondered what we could do with more money. o~ II. OPERATIONS A. Editorial/Monitorial 1. Publications Procurement -- February saw the resumption of several PRC newspaper and journal subscriptions that had been in jeopardy the month before. The problems began when both the Consulate Press Procurement Unit and its counterpart in Beijing inexplicably allowed several subscriptions to lapse. After much bureau intercession and coordination, the papers started arriving on schedule at the beginning of February, including one title, NINGXIA RIBAO, we had been unable to obtain. HUBEI RIBAO, however, is no longer available. No one is sure why or how long this situation will last but political problems in Hubei Province have been suggested as a possible cause. 2. Service to Embassy Beijing -- On 7 February, the bureau received an urgent phone call from Embassy Beijing political officer Charles Martin requesting the widely reported Reuter interviews with PRC Vice Premier Wan Li and Vice Foreign Minister Zhong Xidong. The interviews had created interest for, among other things, Wan's reference to Deng Xiaoping's retreat to the "second line" and Zhong's reference to a "crisis" Approved For Release 2008/04/01 :CIA-RDP86-000408000100050009-1 Approved For Release 2008/04/01 :CIA-RDP86-000408000100050009-1 in Sino-U.S. relations.
