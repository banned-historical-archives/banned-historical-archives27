Memo Re: Gorbachev selection ~ 3rd March 1985 Memo to: Politburo, Central Committee From: KGB Directorate, Desinformatsiya Re: Comrade Gorbachev selection as Party Secretary .. 1. Since it has been decided by Soviet Party law that Comrade Gorbachev is to succeed Comrade Chernenko, we recommend the following approaches to influence development of public opinion in the West, particplarly the United States.* 2. The.most important first step is to announce Comrade Gorbachev's selection as soon as possible - within hours after Comrade Chernenko steps down. Such an announcement would affirm the unbreathable stabil- ? ity of our Marxist-Leninist system and avoid needless speculation about the succession among those inveterate Western idlers who call themselves., Sovietologists. 3. We must show that Comrade Gorbachev is a new broom, not only young and vigorous but also one who brings a fresh breath of air to a world stifled by imperialism and colonialism. He personifies the New Detente. 4. Comrade Joe Adamov is to arrange as a gesture of good will to the American media a live satellite interview as soon as Tass announces Com- rade Gorbachev's selection. In the likely event that President Reagan declines to come to Moscow for the. obsequies for Comrade Chernenko, we must emphasize to the Western media that Prime Minister Thatcher will attend as head of state, thereby disparaging President Reagan. Of course we know well that in Britain, the Queen is head of state while Thatcher is. only head of government. In America, the president is both head of state and head-of government. But the correspondent who will interview Com- rade Adamov, one can be certain, is ignorant of these simple facts.** Thus we can show, by invidious comparison of Reagan and Thatcher, Reagan's heartlessness and his rabid anti-communism. ?
