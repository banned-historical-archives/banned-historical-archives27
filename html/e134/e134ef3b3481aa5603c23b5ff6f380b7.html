Approved For Release 2008/04/02 : CIA-RDP86-0004OR000100110009-4 FOREIGN BROADCAST INFORMATION SERVICE UNITED STATES FORCES, JAPAN OKINAWA BUREAU APO SAN FRANCISCO 96239 MOK-2023 2 March 1982 MEMORANDUM FOR: Director, FBIS THROUGH: Chief, Operations Group SUBJECT: Monthly Report--Okinawa Bureau--February 1982 went to Manila to set up a Headquarters levied project to provide samplings of Philippine regional radios with appropriate Station and Embassy officers. Finally, I visited Hong Kong and Bangkok bureaus for orientation and coordination discussions. In Bangkok I had fruitful discussions with senior officers of the Regional Administrative Management Center (RAMC). B. The exchange rate, which started the month at x?230.30 to the dollar, climbed to Y241.10 by mid-month, the highest since August 1981. By the end of the month the rate had dipped to Y236.60. A. Editorial/Monitorial 1. After coordination with London Bureau, we processed three items from the 8 February Moscow TV "International Panorama" program with IZVESTIYA political observer Aleksandr Bovin. On the 23rd we processed an Army-Navy Day speech by Marshal Nikolay Vasilyevich Ogarkov, chief of the USSR Armed Forces General Staff. We now have responsibility for the Hanoi Domestic Vietnamese 1000-1015 GMT Press Review program, and Bangkok has assumed responsibility for the Hanoi Domestic Vietnamese 1200-1215 GMT program for the VPA. 4. As a result of the Bureau Chief's visit to Hong Kong Bureau, new, simplified, coordination procedures have been set up between the two bureaus regarding coverage and processing of PRC central-press-attributed items from XINHUA and the Beijing Domestic Service. 5. Beijing Domestic Service on 18 February reported Deng Xiaoping's first public appearance in Beijing since 12 January, thus ending weeks of speculation about his where- abouts and standing in the CCP and government. B. Special Services 1. Major Grevencamp from Torii Station visited the bureau on 25 February, returning tapes of Chinese recordings given his office last spring and requesting a further service. He asked if we could provide Russian-language recordings for the Army Intelligence and Security Command (AISC). The tapes will ultimately wend their way into the hands of Army reserve units for training purposes. We agreed that AISC could deal with us directly. They will periodically send us new tapes which we will either fill or exchange about-to-be-erased tapes from our inventory. 2. During the bureau chief's TDY in Tokyo he made arrangements with the 500th Military Intelligence Group, Camp Zama to forward selected videocassettes from the Moscow domestic television service monitored via satellite. The first videocassettes were sent on 2 March; the consumer in turn will be exchanging videocassettes on a one-for-one basis.
