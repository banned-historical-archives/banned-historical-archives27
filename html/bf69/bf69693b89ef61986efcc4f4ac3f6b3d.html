LIAISON Called Marsha, House Appropriations Committee staff, and advised her that we had scheduled Messrs. Charles Snodgrass and Ralph Preston, of the staff, to have lunch with the Director on 13 April at 12:30 p.m. I asked her to find out if they would require transportation and, if so, to let me know. She said she would get back to me. 2. LEGISLATION Called Gil Bray, Minority Counsel of the Subcommittee on International Finance of the Senate Banking Committee, regarding S. 69, a bill to amend the Export Administration Act. He stated that section 107 which would have required certain reports to be made to the Secretary of Commerce has been deleted. 3. LEGISLATION Talked to Ron Kenlien, OMB, and asked him what the Administration's position is on H. R. 3829, a bill "To establish a Commission on Ethics and Financial Disclosure for officers and Employees of the Federal Government, and for other purposes," and the status of the Executive order on the personal conduct and financial disclosure for Government employees. On H. R. 3829, he referred me to Hilda Schreiber, OMB. As for the Executive order, it was not going anywhere. 4. LEGISLATION Attended the mark-up 7 23 0 7 session by the Senate Committee on Banking, Housing and Urban Affairs on S. 305, Senator William Proxmire's (D., Wis.) bill to expand corporate reporting requirements and to outlaw foreign bribery. also attended. The amendments proposed by the Agency were introduced by Senator Adlai Stevenson (D. , Ill.) and adopted by the Committee.
