They protect the youth, they encourage them to advance; they educate them to become combatants in the struggle for the establishment of the new so- ciety. How does the CCP lead the youth to advance? First, it leads them ideolog- ically. The CCP educates the youth in the ideologies of Marxism-Leninism and in Mao Tse-tung's thoughts, The latter are the synthesis of Marxism-Leninism with the revolutionary practices of China. Marxism-Leninism and Mao Tse-tung's thoughts are the most advanced revolutionary theories and ideologies. The CCP does not educate the youth for selfish reasons and fur the benefit of the few, but it endeavors wholeheartedly to educate them to serve the laboring people. It educates the youth to resist injustice, unfairness, and oppression, to love the fatherland fervently, and to bear enmity toward the enemy of the people. The CCP persistently encourages and educates youths to become combat soldiersin the struggle for the establishment of a benevolent and happy new society. As a result, many of the CCP-trained-and-educated youths became heroes and model workers during the people's liberation war and in the country's various recon- struction projects. Second, the CCP leads the youth politically. All CCP policies are focused on benefiting the Chinese people and youth. During the various revolutions in the past, the CCP educated youths to participate. This resulted in winning freedom and a better future for the Chinese people. Without their participa- tion the Chinese people might have been placed in slavery.
